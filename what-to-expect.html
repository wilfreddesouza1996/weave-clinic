Before we meet

You don't need to prepare a speech. But here's what helps.

  • Fill out a short form

    Tell us a little about what's been going on. A few sentences is enough — this just helps us come prepared. No medical jargon required.

  • We'll confirm over WhatsApp

    Once you send the form, we'll reach out within 24 hours to confirm your appointment time and send you a secure video link.

  • Find a quiet spot

    That's it. You don't need to be anywhere special — just somewhere you can talk without being overheard. Your bedroom works fine.

What your first appointment looks like

First appointments run 60 minutes. Here's how they usually go.

  • 1

    We start with you, not your symptoms.

    We want to understand who you are first — your life, your context, what matters to you. The clinical stuff comes after. We're not just gathering a history; we're trying to understand a person.

  • 2

    We go through what's been happening.

    You'll have space to describe what's been going on in your own words — at your own pace. We'll ask questions, but we won't rush you or interrupt. If you need to stop, we stop.

  • 3

    We share what we're thinking — out loud.

    We'll tell you what we're noticing and what questions it's raising for us. Not a diagnosis handed down from on high — more like thinking together. We'll explain our reasoning and invite yours.

  • 4

    We talk about what might help.

    If medication is relevant, we'll explain what it does, what it doesn't do, and what the alternatives are. Nothing is prescribed without your understanding and consent. If therapy is the better first step, we'll say so clearly.

  • 5

    We agree on a next step together.

    You'll leave knowing exactly what we're doing and why. Not a vague plan — a concrete next step you actually feel okay about. If you're not sure about something, that's the time to say so.

What happens after

The first appointment is the beginning, not the assessment.

  • A clear plan, in writing

    After your appointment, you'll receive a brief summary of what we discussed and the plan we agreed on — sent over WhatsApp or email. Nothing clinical or jargon-heavy. Just: here's where we are, here's where we're going.

  • Follow-ups that actually follow

    We check in. If something we recommended isn't working, or something's changed, we adjust. You won't be left to figure it out alone between appointments. Most follow-ups are 30 minutes.

  • Your pace, your timeline

    Some people need weekly appointments for a while. Others find monthly check-ins are enough. We don't lock you into a fixed schedule. We figure out what actually works for your life — and we revisit it.

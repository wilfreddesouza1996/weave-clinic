# KB: The Seven Principles for Making Marriage Work
> John Gottman & Nan Silver | 2nd Edition, 2015 | Harmony Books

---

## Core Framework

Gottman's approach is empirically derived from 40+ years of longitudinal research at the University of Washington's "Love Lab," studying 700+ couples across seven separate studies. The core finding: **marital success depends not on conflict resolution skill but on the quality of the couple's friendship**. Gottman can predict divorce with 91% accuracy by observing a couple interact for as little as 15 minutes.

The model rests on three pillars:
1. **Positive Sentiment Override (PSO)** -- when the friendship is strong, partners give each other the benefit of the doubt; neutral or ambiguous messages are interpreted positively. When PSO breaks down, Negative Sentiment Override takes over: even positive gestures are read as hostile.
2. **The Sound Relationship House** -- a multi-level architectural metaphor for the structure of a successful marriage, with trust and commitment as weight-bearing walls.
3. **The 5:1 Ratio** -- stable, happy marriages maintain at least 5 positive interactions for every 1 negative interaction. This ratio holds during conflict discussions. Couples heading for divorce fall below this threshold, often approaching 0.8:1.

Key departure from traditional therapy: **active listening and conflict resolution alone do not save marriages**. Hahlweg's Munich study and Jacobson's own data showed only 35-50% success rates with relapse to 18-25% at one year. Gottman's approach targets friendship, trust, and shared meaning, not just communication skills.

## Chapter Digest

### Chapter 1: Inside the Seattle Love Lab
Introduces the Love Lab methodology -- couples observed overnight in a fabricated apartment with video cameras, microphones, and physiological sensors (heart rate, blood velocity, sweat output, endocrine/immune function). Gottman predicts divorce with 91% accuracy across seven studies; in one newlywed study, 98% accuracy. Debunks myths: neuroses don't ruin marriages if partners accommodate; common interests don't help if interaction is hostile; affairs are symptoms not causes (80% of divorced cite growing apart, not infidelity); men are not biologically unfit for monogamy; men and women are not from different planets -- for both sexes, the quality of friendship determines 70% of satisfaction with sex, romance, and passion.

### Chapter 2: What Does Make Marriage Work?
Establishes that happy marriages are based on deep friendship -- mutual respect and enjoyment of each other's company. Introduces **Positive Sentiment Override (PSO)** and the "set point" analogy. Introduces the **Sound Relationship House** with trust and commitment as walls. Defines **attunement** as the core emotional connection. Introduces **repair attempts** as the secret weapon of emotionally intelligent couples. Introduces the **Roach Motel for Lovers** -- chronic distrust where couples check in and can't get out. States that 69% of marital problems are perpetual and cannot be resolved; the key is dialogue, not solution. Introduces the concept that most quarrels are not about the surface issue but about deeper hidden issues.

### Chapter 3: How I Predict Divorce
The six signs that predict divorce: (1) **Harsh Start-Up** -- 96% of the time, the outcome of a conversation can be predicted from the first 3 minutes; (2) **The Four Horsemen** -- Criticism, Contempt, Defensiveness, Stonewalling; (3) **Flooding** -- physiological overwhelm (HR > 100 bpm, fight-or-flight activation); (4) **Body Language** -- physiological stress markers; (5) **Failed Repair Attempts** -- Four Horsemen alone predict divorce at 82%; adding failed repairs pushes accuracy into the 90s; (6) **Bad Memories** -- couples rewrite their history negatively. The end-stage trajectory: problems seem severe -> talking seems useless -> parallel lives -> loneliness -> potential affair. Emotionally distant couples divorce after average 16 years vs. 5.6 years for overtly conflictual couples.

### Chapter 4: Principle 1 -- Enhance Your Love Maps
A **love map** is the part of your brain where you store all relevant information about your partner's life -- their worries, hopes, likes, dislikes, friends, stresses. Emotionally intelligent couples constantly update these maps. Couples with detailed love maps weathered the transition to parenthood (67% of couples experience precipitous drop in satisfaction after first baby; 33% did not, and they had detailed love maps). Exercises: Love Map 20 Questions Game, Open-Ended Questions, "Who Am I?" deep-dive questionnaire covering triumphs, injuries, emotional world, mission/legacy.

### Chapter 5: Principle 2 -- Nurture Your Fondness and Admiration
Fondness and admiration are the antidote to contempt. If completely absent, the marriage cannot be saved. Test: how couples describe their early history -- 94% of the time, couples who put a positive spin on history and partner's character will have a happy future. Introduces **cherishing** -- the habit of maximizing positive thoughts about your partner when apart and minimizing negative ones. Research by Robinson and Price found unhappy couples only noticed 50% of positive interactions that objective observers counted. Exercises: "I Appreciate..." list, History and Philosophy of Your Relationship questionnaire, Cherishing checklist, Seven-Week Course in Fondness and Admiration (daily thought + task, 5 days/week).

### Chapter 6: Principle 3 -- Turn Toward Each Other Instead of Away
Romance is fueled by small daily moments of connection, not grand gestures. Introduces **bids** for attention, affection, humor, or support. In 6-year follow-up of newlyweds: couples who remained married turned toward bids 86% of the time; those who divorced averaged only 33%. Each "turning toward" funds the **emotional bank account**. Two obstacles: (1) Missing bids wrapped in anger -- search for the plea underneath harsh words; (2) Electronic distraction -- culture of distraction undermines attentive presence. Key exercise: **Stress-Reducing Conversation** -- 20-30 minutes daily, talking about life outside the marriage; cardinal rule: understanding must precede advice. "When you are in pain, the world stops and I listen."

### Chapter 7: Principle 4 -- Let Your Partner Influence You
When a man is unwilling to share power with his partner, there is an 81% chance the marriage will self-destruct. Data show 65% of men escalate their wives' negativity via the Four Horsemen rather than matching or de-escalating. Women already accept influence in the vast majority of marriages, including unstable ones. About 35% of men studied are emotionally intelligent husbands who honor and respect their wives. Gender difference rooted in childhood play: girls' games focus on relationships; boys' games on competition. By age 7, cross-gender friendships drop to near zero (Maccoby). The emotionally intelligent husband has revised his definition of success to prioritize family. "Yield to win" -- the metaphor of navigating traffic by driving around obstacles rather than demanding they move.

### Chapter 8: The Two Kinds of Marital Conflict
All conflicts are either **perpetual** (69%) or **solvable** (31%). Perpetual problems arise from fundamental personality or value differences and will never fully resolve. Happy couples cope with perpetual problems using humor, affection, and acceptance -- like a chronic health condition managed with good strategies. When perpetual problems become **gridlocked**, the couple spins their wheels, feels rejected, and eventually disengages emotionally. Gridlock signs: no humor/affection in discussions, increasing entrenchment, vilification, emotional disengagement. Solvable problems are situational, less gut-wrenching, not symbolic of deeper issues. A solvable problem left unresolved can evolve into a gridlocked perpetual problem. Dan Wile: "When choosing a long-term partner, you will inevitably be choosing a particular set of unsolvable problems."

### Chapter 9: Principle 5 -- Solve Your Solvable Problems
Five steps: (1) **Soften start-up** -- four parts: accept some responsibility + state feelings + describe specific situation + state positive need; 96% of the time, conversations end on the same note they begin; (2) **Make and receive repair attempts** -- scripted phrases in categories: I Feel, I Need to Calm Down, Sorry, Get to Yes, Stop Action; success depends on marital friendship, not eloquence; (3) **Soothe yourself and each other** -- self-soothing when HR exceeds 100 bpm, take 20+ minute break with relaxing activity; (4) **Compromise** -- use the Two-Circle Method (inner circle = non-negotiables, outer circle = areas of flexibility); (5) **Process grievances** -- "emotional injuries" from how you fought, not what you fought about; 7-step exercise including identifying triggers rooted in enduring vulnerabilities and childhood. Taking responsibility is "manna from heaven" for the listener.

### Chapter 10: Coping with Typical Solvable Problems
Covers six common conflict areas with specific strategies: (1) **Work stress** -- stress-reducing conversation, leave work stress at the door; (2) **In-laws** -- spouse must side with partner over family of origin, establish boundaries together; (3) **Money** -- three-step budgeting process (itemize, manage daily, plan future), work as a team; (4) **Sex** -- redefine sex as everything positive between you ("Every positive thing you do in your relationship is foreplay"), learn to talk about it gently, chart sexual love maps, ongoing conversations; (5) **Housework** -- men must do more; women find a man's willingness to do housework "extremely erotic"; the key is the wife's subjective view of fairness, not a strict 50/50 split; (6) **New baby** -- 67% of couples experience a large drop in satisfaction; those who maintain friendship and love maps are protected.

### Chapter 11: Principle 6 -- Overcome Gridlock
The key: **unrequited dreams are at the core of every gridlocked conflict**. The endless argument symbolizes a profound difference that needs honoring, not solving. Dreams often go unspoken because partners feel unentitled to them or fear they're incompatible with the relationship. Process: (1) Become a "dream detective" -- uncover hidden dreams behind entrenched positions; (2) Speaker describes the dream, its origins, its meaning; Listener asks the "Dream Catcher's Magic Questions" without judgment; (3) Three levels of honoring dreams: understanding/interest, actively enabling, becoming part of the dream; (4) Two-Circle Method for temporary compromise; (5) Say thank you. The goal is not to solve the problem but to defang it -- move from gridlock to dialogue. "Couples who are demanding of their marriage are more likely to have deeply satisfying unions than those who lower their expectations."

### Chapter 12: Principle 7 -- Create Shared Meaning
The highest level of the Sound Relationship House. Four pillars: (1) **Rituals of Connection** -- formal and informal shared activities imbued with meaning (mealtimes, holidays, bedtimes, date nights, vacations, how you greet/part/reunite); (2) **Support for Each Other's Roles** -- shared philosophy about what it means to be a spouse, parent, worker, friend; (3) **Shared Goals** -- deeper aspirations beyond the practical, working together toward common purpose; (4) **Shared Values and Symbols** -- philosophical tenets, family stories, meaningful objects. Each pillar has a questionnaire and exercises. Creating shared meaning turns marriage from an institution into a culture of two.

### Afterword: What Now?
The "Magic Six Hours" -- weekly investments that maintain the marriage: (1) Partings (2 min/day x 5 = 10 min) -- learn one thing happening in partner's day; (2) Reunions (20 min/day x 5 = 1h40m) -- stress-reducing conversation; (3) Admiration and appreciation (5 min/day x 7 = 35 min) -- find genuine affection and appreciation daily; (4) Affection (5 min/day x 7 = 35 min) -- kiss, hold, touch; (5) Weekly date (2 hours); (6) State of the Union meeting (1 hour/week) -- discuss what went well, process emotional injuries, focus on appreciation. Also introduces the **Marital Poop Detector** -- catching small issues before they escalate.

## The Seven Principles (Detailed)

### 1. Enhance Your Love Maps
- **Concept**: The cognitive room you make for your marriage. Knowing your partner's world in detail.
- **Why it matters**: Couples with detailed love maps are far better prepared for stressful events. 67% of new parents see satisfaction plummet; the 33% who don't had strong love maps (Shapiro study).
- **Exercises**: Love Map 20 Questions Game (60 weighted questions); Open-Ended Questions (30 deep questions); "Who Am I?" (5-section: Triumphs, Injuries, Emotional World, Mission/Legacy, Becoming).
- **Questionnaire**: 20 T/F items; 10+ = strength.

### 2. Nurture Fondness and Admiration
- **Concept**: The reservoir of positive regard. If absent, marriage is unsalvageable. If buried, it can be revived.
- **The test**: How couples describe early history. 94% predictive accuracy.
- **Key insight**: Unhappy couples miss 50% of positive interactions (Robinson & Price).
- **Exercises**: "I Appreciate..." (5 from list + examples); History and Philosophy of Your Marriage (13 parts); Cherishing (10 qualities from 100+ word list); Seven-Week Course (daily thought + task).
- **Questionnaire**: 20 T/F items; 10+ = strength.

### 3. Turn Toward Each Other Instead of Away
- **Concept**: Bids for connection. Partners respond by turning toward or turning away.
- **Data**: Married couples turned toward 86% vs. 33% for divorced.
- **Emotional bank account**: Cumulative deposits from everyday moments.
- **Key exercise**: Stress-Reducing Conversation (20-30 min daily; 8 guidelines).
- **Haim Ginott's rule**: "Understanding must precede advice."
- **Questionnaire**: 20 T/F items; 12+ = strength.

### 4. Let Your Partner Influence You
- **Data**: 81% chance of self-destruction when man refuses to share power. 65% of men escalate via Four Horsemen.
- **Gender roots**: Boys accept influence only from boys from age 1.5; girls from both (Maccoby). By age 7, cross-gender play drops to zero.
- **Emotionally intelligent husband**: ~35% of men. Revised definition of success.
- **Exercises**: Yield to Win; Gottman Island Survival Game.
- **Questionnaire**: 20 items; 6+ = strength.

### 5. Solve Your Solvable Problems
Five steps:
1. **Soften start-up** (4 parts: responsibility + feelings + specific situation + positive need)
2. **Make and receive repair attempts** (57 scripted phrases in 5 categories)
3. **Soothe yourself and each other** (HR > 100 bpm = 20+ min break)
4. **Compromise** (Two-Circle Method: inner = non-negotiable, outer = flexible)
5. **Process grievances** (7-step emotional injury exercise)

### 6. Overcome Gridlock
- **Core insight**: Unrequited dreams at the core of every gridlocked conflict.
- **Dream Catcher's Magic Questions**: "What do you believe?", "What does your position mean to you?", "Tell me the story of your dream", "What do you want/need?"
- **Three levels of honoring dreams**: understand, enable, become part of
- **Goal**: Move from gridlock to dialogue, not resolution.

### 7. Create Shared Meaning
Four pillars: (1) Rituals of Connection (questionnaire: 13 items); (2) Support for Roles (7 items); (3) Shared Goals (10 items); (4) Shared Values and Symbols (20 items).

## Research Findings

### The 5:1 Ratio
Stable, happy marriages maintain at least **5 positive interactions for every 1 negative** during conflict discussions. Couples heading for divorce fall below this threshold.

### Prediction Accuracy
- 91% average across seven studies; 98% in one newlywed study (15/17 divorces over 7 years)
- Four Horsemen alone: 82% accuracy. Adding failed repair attempts: 90%+
- 84% of newlyweds high on Four Horsemen but who repaired effectively were stable and happy 6 years later

### Flooding / Diffuse Physiological Arousal (DPA)
- HR > 100 bpm (up to 165); adrenaline surge; fight-or-flight response
- Males more physiologically reactive, slower to recover (evolutionary basis)
- Kiecolt-Glaser & Glaser: future-divorced newlyweds had 34% higher adrenaline and 2x ACTH during arguments

### Perpetual vs. Solvable Problems
- **69% perpetual** (fundamental differences); **31% solvable** (situational)
- At 4-year follow-ups, same arguments persisting
- Happy couples manage perpetual problems with humor and acceptance; unhappy couples gridlock

### Sentiment Override
- PSO (Robert Weiss, U. Oregon): positive thoughts supersede negative feelings
- NSO: everything interpreted negatively; neutral cues read as hostile

### Gender Differences in Conflict
- 85% of heterosexual stonewallers are men (cardiovascular reactivity)
- 80% of the time, wives bring up sticky issues
- 65% of men escalate wives' negativity via Four Horsemen
- 81% chance of marriage failure when man refuses to share power

### Bids for Connection
- Married couples turned toward 86% of the time; divorced 33%

### Transition to Parenthood
- 67% drop in satisfaction after first baby; 33% protected by detailed love maps (Shapiro)

### Health Effects
- Unhappy marriage: +35% sickness risk, -4 to -8 years life expectancy (Verbrugge & House)
- Contemptuous couples suffer more infectious illness
- Happily married: greater WBC proliferation, more effective NK cells
- Children in high-hostility homes: elevated stress hormones, more truancy, depression, aggression by age 15

### Workshop Outcomes
- 75% success at 12-month follow-up (640 couples); 20% relapse (vs. 30-50% standard)
- At-risk dropped from 27% to 7% (to 4% with additional therapy)
- Prevention workshops 3x more effective than intervention for troubled couples

> **EXAM PEARL:** The **Four Horsemen** arrive in order: **Criticism -> Contempt -> Defensiveness -> Stonewalling**. Contempt is the single best predictor of divorce and is fueled by long-simmering negative thoughts.

> **EXAM PEARL:** **69% of marital problems are perpetual**. The difference between happy and unhappy couples is not whether they have perpetual problems but whether they can dialogue about them with humor and acceptance or become gridlocked.

> **EXAM PEARL:** **Flooding** (DPA) = HR > 100 bpm during conflict. When flooded, creative problem-solving is physiologically impossible. Only productive response: **20+ minute self-soothing break**.

> **EXAM PEARL:** **Betrayal lies at the heart of every failed relationship** (Gottman). Betrayal = any act that doesn't prioritize the commitment -- not just sexual affairs but emotional distance, siding with parents, disrespect, broken promises.

> **EXAM PEARL:** Active listening alone does not save marriages. Jacobson's best: 35-50% improvement, 18-25% retained at 1 year. Gottman's friendship-based approach: 75% success at 1 year, 20% relapse.

> **EXAM PEARL:** The **Sound Relationship House** has 7 levels (Love Maps -> Fondness/Admiration -> Turning Toward -> PSO -> Manage Conflict -> Life Dreams -> Shared Meaning) with **Trust and Commitment** as walls.

> **CLINICAL ANCHOR:** When assessing a couple, ask about their early history. Positive, vivid recall = fondness/admiration system intact, marriage salvageable. Negative rewriting or inability to remember = poor prognosis. 94% predictive accuracy.

> **CLINICAL ANCHOR:** The Stress-Reducing Conversation (20-30 min daily about external stresses, NOT the marriage) is the single most effective "turning toward" activity. Rules: genuine interest, no unsolicited advice, take partner's side, validate emotions.

> **MNEMONIC: ATTUNE** -- Gottman's acronym for emotional attunement: **A**wareness of partner's emotion, **T**urning toward, **T**olerance of two viewpoints, trying to **U**nderstand, **N**on-defensive responding, **E**mpathy.
